Today I read the H2O paper (Heavy-Hitter Oracle) by Zhang et al. The core idea is elegant — track cumulative attention scores across generation steps and evict KV pairs that consistently receive low attention. Their "heavy hitter" tokens are the ones that accumulate disproportionate attention mass. This gives me a strong baseline to beat with Aegis. The gap I see: H2O still trusts raw attention as a proxy for importance, which Jain & Wallace showed is unreliable. My composite scoring function should outperform on adversarial needle-retrieval tasks where the "needle" token has diffuse attention.
