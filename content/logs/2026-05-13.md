Today the Sponge effect was the topic i went in depth. which i knew at high level as problem of transformer so i didnt went in depth before. but now i understand it heavily. 

so first let me give the type or 2 effect different kind of sponge effect.
1.) Global Sponge (position-based):
First few tokens absorb disproportionate attention from the ENTIRE sequence. "The" at position 0 gets attended to by every single token that comes after it. 

2.)Local Sponge (structural/punctuation-based):
Punctuation tokens like "." and "\n" absorb attention locally — from the tokens immediately around them, at sentence boundaries. Not as extreme as global sinks but still inflated relative to their semantic content.

we have some terms also to know before going in depth:
1.) sink tokens - "The", ".", "\n" 
2.) sparcity - Percentage of attention weights that are exactly zero.

so in terms of this paper we know heavy hitters will be sinks, but it is not true the other way around. also even if the sentence is short

that is where the distinction of H2O and AEGIS comes to picture as H2O takes all high scoring tokens and in Aegis we are trying to distinguish between them, as we wanna keep the semantic heavy hitters and not sinks.
